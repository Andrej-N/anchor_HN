Our House

Four centuries of stone, refined for the way you travel today.

Anchora occupies a 17th-century stone house (built 1680, restored 2014/2024) in the strict centre of Herceg Novi's old town — a building shaped by Venetian, Austro-Hungarian and Mediterranean hands, now carefully reimagined into six distinct apartments. A panoramic terrace looks onto the entrance of the Boka Bay; the open-air stone yard reads like a small museum.

Original walls, hand-hewn timber beams and quiet inner courtyards have been preserved alongside considered modern comforts: linen-soft beds, deep showers, fast Wi-Fi and air conditioning. Rustic in spirit, luxurious in detail.

— It feels like a private home, because it once was.

Where you'll be

In the old town, two minutes from the sea.

Anchora sits inside Herceg Novi's historic centre — the kind of address where everything that matters is on foot. The Adriatic is roughly 200 metres away; the bus station, the bay-front promenade and the medieval walls are minutes from the door.
